# AI & Cybersecurity Glossary

## AI (Artificial Intelligence)
Technology that enables computers to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence.

---

## LLM (Large Language Model)
An AI model trained on large amounts of text data to generate human-like responses.

Examples:
- ChatGPT
- Claude
- Gemini
- Llama

---

## Deepfake
AI-generated media designed to imitate real people through video, audio, or images.

---

## Prompt Injection
An attack where malicious input manipulates an AI system into ignoring instructions or revealing information.

---

## MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)
A security mechanism requiring two or more forms of authentication.

Example:
Password + mobile authenticator app.

---

## Zero Trust
A security model based on the principle:

> Never trust, always verify.

---

## DLP (Data Loss Prevention)
Technologies designed to prevent sensitive information from leaving an organization.

---

## EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response)
Security solutions that detect, investigate, and respond to threats on devices.

---

## SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)
Platforms that collect, analyze, and correlate security logs.

Examples:
- Splunk
- Wazuh
- Microsoft Sentinel

---

## PKI (Public Key Infrastructure)
A framework that enables secure communications through certificates, encryption, and trusted authorities.

---

## TLS (Transport Layer Security)
The modern protocol used to secure communications on the internet.

Used by:
- HTTPS websites
- APIs
- Secure applications

---

## CA (Certificate Authority)
A trusted organization responsible for issuing digital certificates.

---

## OCSP (Online Certificate Status Protocol)
A protocol used to verify certificate validity in real time.

---

## CRL (Certificate Revocation List)
A list of certificates that should no longer be trusted.

---

## Ransomware
Malware that encrypts files and demands payment for their recovery.

---

## IOC (Indicator of Compromise)
Evidence that suggests malicious activity has occurred.

Examples:
- Hidden administrator accounts
- Encoded PowerShell commands
- Large outbound traffic

---

## Persistence
Techniques attackers use to maintain long-term access to systems.

Examples:
- Hidden user accounts
- Scheduled tasks
- Backdoors

---

## SASE (Secure Access Service Edge)
A cloud-based security architecture combining networking and security services.

---

## NIST AI RMF
The NIST Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework.

Core functions:
- Govern
- Map
- Measure
- Manage
