# NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) Summary

## Introduction

The NIST Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) helps organizations develop, deploy, and manage trustworthy AI systems.

The framework encourages organizations to balance innovation with security, privacy, fairness, reliability, and accountability.

---

# The Four Core Functions

The AI RMF consists of four major functions:

## 1. Govern

Governance establishes policies, oversight, and accountability.

Examples include:

- AI policies
- Leadership responsibilities
- Ethical guidelines
- Risk ownership
- Regulatory compliance

### Questions Organizations Should Ask

- Who is responsible for AI decisions?
- How are AI risks monitored?
- What policies guide AI usage?

---

## 2. Map

Mapping identifies:

- Intended uses
- Stakeholders
- Risks
- Dependencies
- Potential impacts

Organizations should understand:

- What the AI system does.
- Who uses it.
- What data it processes.
- What could go wrong.

---

## 3. Measure

Measurement evaluates AI performance and risks.

Examples include:

- Security testing
- Bias assessments
- Reliability analysis
- Privacy reviews
- Model validation

Organizations should continuously monitor:

- Accuracy
- Safety
- Robustness
- Transparency

---

## 4. Manage

Management focuses on responding to identified risks.

Examples include:

- Security controls
- Incident response procedures
- Access management
- Continuous monitoring
- Risk mitigation strategies

Risk management is an ongoing process rather than a one-time activity.

---

# Cybersecurity Connections

The AI RMF aligns closely with traditional cybersecurity principles.

Examples include:

## Zero Trust

Organizations should continuously verify:

- Users
- Devices
- Applications
- AI systems

---

## Defense in Depth

Multiple layers of protection remain essential:

- MFA
- DLP
- EDR
- SIEM
- Security Awareness Training

---

## Risk Management

Organizations must balance:

- Innovation
- Security
- Privacy
- Compliance
- Ethical considerations

---

# Why the AI RMF Matters

The framework helps organizations:

- Build trustworthy AI systems.
- Improve transparency.
- Reduce cybersecurity risks.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Maintain regulatory compliance.
- Strengthen governance processes.

---

# Key Takeaways

The four AI RMF functions are:

1. Govern
2. Map
3. Measure
4. Manage

Successful AI adoption requires:

- Strong leadership
- Continuous monitoring
- Effective security controls
- Responsible risk management
- Human oversight

AI security is not only a technical challenge—it is also a governance and organizational challenge.
